LOVIN' "LATIN MADNESS" - A review by Danita

Smokin' Hot was recently invited to attend LATIN MADNESS by one of our S.H.E. supporters in the show. I finally got to go last weekend and was I ever glad I did. Not only was I pleasantly surprised by the show that I got, I was equally surprised by what I took away. Being billed as a "dancical", LATIN MADNESS is definitely that and so much more. With a balanced mix of dancing, comedy and, to my surprise, history, I was transported through the many years and eras of Latin dance. Everything from African dance to rhumba, salsa, disco and beyond was highlighted in incredible dance numbers. The pulsing drive of the live pit orchestra only further enhanced our listening and viewing pleasure.

I could go on and on with details like the timbale face-off reminiscent of the times Tito Puente took the stage or the hilarious comedy sets that set the stage for the show, but I'd rather not ruin this gem for you. This is definitely something for not only Latinos to see, but for anyone who has an appreciation for music and the power of music to make you dance. LATIN MADNESS is definitely something new, fresh and vividly entertaining. One can only hope that Broadway pays attention and gives this show the exposure it truly needs.

Don't take it from us. As their press release touts: "The twenty five member LATIN MADNESS dance troupe is comprised of an international array of dancers that continue to be on the cutting edge in the world of Latin Dance. These performers are as versatile as they are good. Whether dancing Mambo, Cha-Cha, Merengue or Hip-Hop they take the hot pulsating Latin beats and combine them with their fast stepping, gyrating bodies for a dance extravaganza the likes of which will have you asking a for a Mojito to cool off. These performers have toured internationally in countries such as England, Italy, France, Japan, Israel, Turkey, Holland, Toronto, Switzerland, Puerto Rico, Colombia, Belgium and Peru. In the United States they have toured cities such as Los Angeles, Chicago, Miami, Houston, Atlanta, Washington D.C. and the list goes on."

Come and see for yourself why this show will have you dancing all night! Shows will run through November 2nd at the Hostos Center for the Arts & Cluture (450 Grand Concourse, Bronx). Tickets are on sale now for $40-45. AND JUST ADDED: $20 balcony seating!
