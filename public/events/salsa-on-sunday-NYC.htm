SALSA ON SUNDAYS NYC CON SABOR

@ DARDO GALLETTO STUDIO

   

See Promotional Video

DATE OF EVENT Usually held on the 4th Sunday of the month, but on some months that date changes, so see our Main Calendar for exact dates.
TIME Usually 6:00 PM - 10:00 PM.  See our Main Calendar for exact time on a particular date, or the flyer posted above. 

LOCATION

 Held at Dardo Galletto Studios, 151 West 46 Street (between Broadway/7th Avenue and 6th Avenue),  11th floor, NYC 10036.   Located in the heart of Manhattan's midtown Times Square theater district, right next door to Havana Central Restaurant.  Studio phone 212-575-0222.
VIEW LOCATION MAP Click Here For Map.  Travel instructions are below.
PRESENTED BY Edison Hidalgo.
CONTACT INFO

Edison Hidalgo - Email - edisonota@aol.com - Phone 917-816-9902 - Web site  www.SalsaOnSundayNYC.com .
TYPE OF EVENT & VENUE A predominantly On 2 dance event for the mambo dancers held in an attractive, nicely renovated rectangular-shaped studio with an excellent "medium speed" dance floor, mirrored walls, DJ sound system and seating/lounging with an assortment of beverages.
MUSIC Almost entirely pure classic salsa plus a little cha cha, bachata, played on a professional DJ sound system.
DANCE PERFORMANCES Sometimes.  They would be listed on our Main Calendar.
DJ DJ Ray Vega "El Maestro De La Salsa"
BAND None.
DANCE FLOOR Excellent good-size hardwood ballroom-class dance floor which is "medium speed".
PRICES Usually $10 admission.  Prices subject to change.  See flyer above for pricing.
BEVERAGES AND FOOD Cash water and sports beverages.
DRESS CODE Casual.

DANCE TIMING

Almost entirely "New York On 2" timing and style.
DESCRIPTION

Edison Hidalgo presents this classic salsa social held in a big comfortable space with the capacity for a lot of couples dancing together.  There's room for plenty of dancers plus chairs surrounding the dance floor for relaxing and socializing.  He has created this event to appeal to the pure On 2 salsa lovers, so they can have a downtown venue to dance in and hang out with other salseros.  The crowd is nicely diverse in terms of age, their dance background, where they live, how long they've been on our NY salsa scene, but they all have one thing in common:  they love mambo dancing.  DJ Ray Vega "El Maestro De La Salsa"  plays plenty of classic salsa plus a little cha cha, bachata & more .

Edison Hidalgo has both been on the scene for quite a while and attends the events of several different On 2 studios, and his goal is to bring everyone together for a great night of dancing.  His especially known for his photos and videos of salsa dancing taking place in many locations in the NYC mambo community.
 
HOW TO GET THERE

Held at Dardo Galletto Studios, 151 West 46 Street (between Broadway/7th Avenue and 6th Avenue),  11th floor   Located in the heart of Manhattan's midtown Times Square theater district, right next door to Havana Central Restaurant.  Click Here For Map .

By Train:  Take the B, D, F, M trains to the 47th-50th St. Rockefeller Center Station; or the 1, 2, 3 to 42nd St./Times Square Station; or the A, C, E trains to 42nd St./Port Authority Station; or the N, Q, R to the 42nd or 49th Street Stations. It's just a short walk from any of these stations to the studio.
